# Social Media Optimization Reference

## Platform Guidelines

### LinkedIn
- Length: 1,300-2,000 characters performs best; first 2 lines must hook before "see more"
- Format: short paragraphs (1-2 sentences), line breaks between ideas, no walls of text
- Hashtags: 3-5, mix of broad + niche
- Image: 1200x627px (link posts), 1080x1080px (native image posts)
- Best posting times: Tue-Thu, 8-10am and 12-1pm (audience local time)
- Algorithm factors: dwell time, early comments in first hour, "see more" click-through

### X / Twitter
- Length: single post ≤280 chars; threads for longer ideas (5-8 posts)
- Format: front-load the point, one idea per post in a thread
- Hashtags: 0-2 max, overuse reads as spammy
- Image: 1600x900px (16:9)
- Best posting times: weekdays 9am-12pm and 5-6pm
- Algorithm factors: replies > reposts > likes, avoid external links in the first post

### Instagram
- Caption length: first 125 characters shown before "more" — hook there
- Format: line breaks, emoji as visual bullets, question at the end to drive comments
- Hashtags: 5-15 relevant tags (mix broad, niche, branded), place in caption or first comment
- Image: 1080x1350px (4:5 portrait performs best in feed)
- Reels: 1080x1920px (9:16), hook in first 1-2 seconds, captions burned in
- Best posting times: weekdays 11am-1pm and 7-9pm

### Facebook
- Length: 40-80 characters gets highest engagement for link posts
- Format: conversational, question-driven, native video outperforms links
- Image: 1200x630px (link), 1080x1080px (native)
- Best posting times: weekdays 1-4pm
- Algorithm factors: meaningful interactions (comments, shares) weighted over likes

### TikTok
- Video length: 21-34 seconds sweet spot for completion rate
- Format: hook in first 1-3 seconds, on-screen text, trending audio when relevant
- Hashtags: 3-5, mix trending + niche + branded
- Best posting times: weekdays 6-10am and 7-11pm
- Algorithm factors: completion rate and rewatches matter more than likes

## Hashtag Strategy

1. **Branded** (1) — your own campaign/company tag for tracking and community
2. **Niche** (2-3) — specific to your exact topic, lower competition, higher relevance
3. **Broad** (1-2) — category-level reach, higher competition
4. Rotate hashtag sets across posts — reusing the exact same set every time can
   suppress reach on platforms that flag repetitive patterns

## Engagement Elements Checklist

- Question at the end of the caption/post
- Poll or "this or that" for Stories/Community tab
- Clear single CTA (comment, save, share — pick one per post)
- Native format (video, carousel) over external links where the platform rewards it
- Respond to comments within the first hour to boost algorithmic signal

## Analytics to Track Per Platform

- Reach vs. impressions (discovery vs. frequency)
- Engagement rate = (likes + comments + shares) / reach
- Save rate (Instagram/TikTok — strong intent signal)
- Click-through rate (link-driving posts)
- Follower growth rate week-over-week
- Best-performing content pillar and format, reviewed monthly
